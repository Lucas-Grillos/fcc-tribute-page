The Life of Louis Pasteur

The Greatest Man to Ever Live

A timeline of Louis Pasteur's life

  • 1822 - Louis was born on the 29th of December, in Dole, France.
  • 1832 - At the age of 9, Louis showed no particular interest as a scientist. He did, however, show aptitude as an artist.
  • 1839 - Louis is accepted into the College Royal at Besancon.
  • 1842 - Graduates with a BA and BSc with honoros in physics, mathematics, Latin, and drawing.
  • 1843 - After passing with a very low score the previous year, Louis achieves high ranks on the entrance exam to the École normale supérieure.
  • 1852 - Louis was appointed the chair of chemistry at the University of Strasbourg, where he had in 1948 been appointed a professor. In 1949, he had married the daughter of the universities rector, Marie Laurent.
  • 1854 - Pasteur is appointed professor of chemistry at the University of Lille. While in Lille, Louis was asked to work on problems at nearby alcohol distillation. This lead him to studying issues related to the souring of milk, caused by compounds such as lactic acid.
  • 1857 - His previous studies lead Louis to coin the term Pasteur Effect; the unexpected discovery the fermentation process could be arrested by passing oxygen through the fermenting fluid.
  • 1863 - Louis is personally requested by the emperor of France, Napoleon III, to investigate wine contamination. Pasteur found it to be caused by microbes. To combat this, he invented a process known as Pasteurization, where the wine was heated 120-140F to kill the microbes. Today, this process is most universally used in milk to prevent the aging process.
  • 1873 - In 1873, Louis Pasteur is appointed an associate member of the Académie de Médecine, to the chagrin of the medical establishment for his advocacy of the germ theory of disease. Nonetheless, over the next decade, Pasteur developed the principle of vaccination and helped contribute the burgeoning field of immunology.
  • 1879 - Pasteur develops a vaccine for chicken cholera, by injecting chickens with an attenuated form of the cultured virus. Later that year, he also began investigating anthrax.
  • 1881 - Louis Pasteur's work on anthrax is vindicated when a batch of sheeps are exposed to the virus. The sheep that had been vaccinated all survived, and the control group all died.
  • 1885 - After several years of researching rabies, Pasteur vaccinates a young boy exposed to the virus by a rabid dog. The result was so successful, that hundreds of other bite victims were subsequently saved by Pasteur's vaccine, launching an era of preventive medicine.
  • 1887 - Louis launches the Pasteur Institute, which he continued to work at until his death in 1895. The Pasteur Institute still exists today, and is responsible helping control diseases such as diptheria, tetanus, tuberculosis, poliomyelitis, influenza, and yellow fever.
